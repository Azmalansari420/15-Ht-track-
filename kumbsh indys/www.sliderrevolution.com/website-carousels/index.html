about Slider Revolution carousels

A carousel is a website component that contains numerous slides, cards, or screens. Visitors are able to scroll or swipe through a carousel to reveal all of the content contained within it.

Carousels are great for promoting content. For example:

That said, our responsive editor and preview mode allow you to customize the interface and user experience for laptops, tablets, and smartphones as much or as little as you want.

Although the terms slider and carousel may be used interchangeably, theres a distinct difference in these two rotating website components.

A carousel is often smaller in stature than sliders. Even if a carousel component fills the entire screen, visitors can see more than one slide or card at a time. Also, carousels tend to be user-controlled vs. set to auto-rotate.

A slider, on the other hand, tends to be a fullscreen rotating component. Generally, only one slide appears at a time. Sliders can be auto-rotating and/or allow for manual control.

Some years ago, people were calling for an end to carousels and sliders. However, that was back when these rotating elements were ugly, buggy, and slow. But carousels remain an effective way to engage visitors and increase click-through rates.
