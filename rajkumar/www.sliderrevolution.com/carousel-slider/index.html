How To Create
A Great Carousel Slider Without Any Code

A carousel can be a great way to show off content in a super engaging way. Thanks to Slider Revolution, you dont need any fancy tools or expert coding skills to create one.

Background

Update the slide backgrounds so they align with your brand colors or fit your visual theme. Change colors, add carousel images and videos, or even apply special effects.

Imagery

Depending on the slider type, you might want to upload an image gallery, embed YouTube videos, pull in recent content from your site, or mix-and-match imagery. You can also apply custom styles to your images change the size, placement, and transition effects, for instance.

Text

When designing carousels, you can place your text on top of your imagery. You can also add a carousel caption element. Regardless of where your text goes, replace the text in your template. Then use the editor to make stylistic edits to the font, colors, weight, and more while youre at it.

Buttons

Update the call-to-action buttons in your carousel so that they look great and are easy for visitors to interact with. Then add custom links to a different slide, section, or single page.

Transitions

While you want to ensure that your carousel slides easily, you dont have to stick to a basic slide or fade transition. Our transition effects give you different ways to animate the slider while also experimenting with dimensional space.

Carousel Controls

Navigation and pagination needs to be added to every carousel you build. Start by disabling the auto-play. Then give your user all the controls they need including previous and next controls, pagination, and touch-enabled swiping.

Responsive

When customizing elements in our templates, its a good idea to check that theyre still responsive. We give you all the tools you need to ensure that theres appropriately sized content in your carousel regardless of how big your users screens are.

Publish

Use the Slider Revolution shortcode to embed your new carousel on a web page. You can also use the Slider Revolution widget available in your page builder to add it.

Although sliders and carousels tend to contain the same type of content and can be used for similar purposes, their structures are different. A slider is typically full-screen, so your visitors will only see one slide at a time.

A carousel, on the other hand, has multiple visible slides. Carousel slide dimensions also vary. It depends on what the purpose of the carousel is. For example, a news carousel tends to be smaller so that it seamlessly fits with the surrounding news highlights. Youd want a portfolio slideshow, however, to take up more space.

Both sliders and carousels are effective ways to present a bunch of content in a set space on a web page. However, carousels are particularly good at building anticipation as the next item and previous item in the slider remain visible.

Slider Revolution doesnt have any limits on the number of slides you can add. It all depends on what the purpose of your carousel element is.

For instance, if youre using it to promote upcoming events, the first slide will contain information on your next event. If your goal is to get as many people to sign up for upcoming events, you probably dont want to add too many slides to the carousel.

You might also consider creating multiple carousels for different types of events, content, or categories. That way, users dont feel like theyre stuck in a never-ending loop of content. Instead, they can focus on the carousel items that are most relevant to what theyre searching for.
